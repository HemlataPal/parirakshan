Nature Conservation:

We always believed that Food is the best medicine!

Definition: "Organic farming, system of crop cultivation employing biological methods of fertilization and pest control as substitutes for chemical fertilizers and pesticides; the latter products are regarded by proponents of organic methods as injurious to health and the environment and unnecessary for successful cultivation." ( source: http://global.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/431991/organic-farming )

The Cause: As we are becoming aware of the harmful effects of pesticides on fruits and vegetables, we all are shifting to organic way of farming. Organic farming methods protects environment, minimize soil degradation and erosion, decrease pollution, optimize biological productivity and promote a sound state of health. For us, it is working in harmony with Nature without harming it. A system where the cycle of balance operates, between production and consumption, nothing is ever a waste; every component plays its role.

Grow your own forest

# Category Name of the Project Project Detail
1 Nature Conservation Grow your own forest Problem statement:

According to World Health Organization (WHO) report on World's most polluted cities, 13 of the top 20 most polluted cities in the world are in India. This has led to fears for the health of our and urging us to "ACT NOW or Perish". Delhi, tops the chart with annual average is 153 ug/m3 (micrograms per cubic meter of air), which is six times the WHO's recommended maximum. At various times of the year, this number spikes to much higher levels.

Solution: "Grow Your Own Forest"

For every problem there is a solution and our indefeasible duty is to stand up and take action. Afforestation in cities is the solution to growing pollution and reversing the habitat of many birds and insects. However, it may sound difficult and raise many doubts on how to do it? On the contrary, it is easy for we have an innovative technique to grow your own forest in your own garden, in your city or even at your workplace! A small forest growing technique that takes ten years to by planting dozens of native species to create a thriving ecosystem that develops in just 10 years. The most important thing to note is that this forest grows 10 times faster than traditional ones, are 30 times as dense and 100 times more biodiverse.

Scope of work for you/ how you can support?

1. Identifying the individuals/ organizations who would like to grow forest on their land/ area
2. Raise funds to initiate, complete and scale up the forest size
3. Volunteer your availability on the site to manage the project

How Parirakshan will support?

1. End to end implementation from tilling the land to plantation and regular check on the growth of the forest
2. Consulting and project management services, on-site consultation and supervision, and software support

Target location:

Cities and hills loosing bio-diversity

Sustainable living

# Category Name of the Project Project Detail
2 Nature Conservation Sustainable living Problem statement:

The global estimates suggest evidences of climate change. Today's civilization setup has pushed us into superficial practices that succumb us to make socially approvable but environmentally denouncing choices. Extraction of large quantities of materials from earth's crust, accumulation of chemical compounds like CO2 and CFCs (Cholorflouro Carbons) in the biosphere, excessive cutting of trees at a faster rate than they can grow and destroying the natural ecosystems to replace them with parking lots, taller buildings etc. has physically inhibited nature's ability to maintain ecological balances.

Solution:

Self sustainability on the fundamental needs - Food, Health, Education, Employment, Renewal energy sources and Shelter. We, at Parirakshan Organica Foundation, strongly believe that sticking back to the basics is the only solution to the hysterical environmental imbalance and is the key to prevent our extinction. We aspire to relive our old days where we would live in sync with nature and our dependencies on non-renewable resources were negligible. This would help us achieve the three tenets of sustainability – the environmental sustainability, the economic sustainability and the social sustainability. At Parirakshan, we follow a six- sphere approach to self-sufficient living: Water, shelter, food, energy (Solar, Electricity, transport), finances (mainly for health and clothing), and community (Including relaxation/entertainment thru Music, Meditation, Exercise). Our target is to minimize our reliance on non-renewable resources and on money eventually. At an experimental stage we have adopted a farm land in a village Neri, Jwarna in Uttarkhand. We plan to set up an example/ a prototype for achieving sustainable living by means of adopting sustainable practices.

Scope of work for you/ how you can support?

1. Aid in the arrangement and management of resources required for Health, Education, Renewal energy sources etc.
2. Extend your support by volunteering your availability at site
3. Help us to raise fund
4. Help us in replicating the model site at another locations/ villages

How Parirakshan will support?

1. Project site development
2. Project site maintenance
3. Consulting and project management services for other sites

Location:

Neri village, Uttarakhand. Replicate it further.

Gomukh Trek - waste management

# Category Name of the Project Project Detail
3 Nature Conservation Gomukh Trek - waste management Problem statement:

One can find some of the most dreaded pollution on the Gomukh trek ( Uttarakhand, India) which is 18 km from Gangotri in the foot hills of Bhagirathi. Huge non-biodegradable plastic waste was simply lying in the slopes of valleys and along the streams. It is the snout of the Gangotri Glacier, from where Bhagirathi River originates. Gomukh is one of the primary sources of the Ganges River. It is a popular Hindu pilgrimage site, along with Gangotri, as well as trekking destination The mushroom growth of hotels in Gangotri, the last motorable road head on the route is responsible for increasing pollution of the Ganga and the surroundings.

Solution:

A team with support of Nehru Mountaineering Institute, Uttarkashi to carry out waste collection and disposal work on an on-going basis and spread awareness among tourists in keeping the Gomukh area clean and being sensitive about not polluting the Nature.

Scope of work for you/ how you can support?

1. Volunteer as a member in the team which would visit Gomukh and collect waste
2. Support in the safe disposal of the waste at the collection site
3. Help raise funds for the project

How Parirakshan will support?

1. Project management
2. Logistical arrangements
3. Statutory clearances and protocols

Location:

Gomukh glacier trek, Uttarakhand, India
